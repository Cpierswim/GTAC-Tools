LSC Record Rundown - Tuesday, April 10, 2012

At the end of the short course season, there was a bevy of LSC records set, 20 to be exact. This year was a bit of a contrast to last year in that this year there were a ton of records broken right at the end of the season and last year there was a steady stream of them coming in all year with no real big spike at any time.

We will begin with the records broken at the Ohio Short Course Junior Olympics.

  • Carson Foster (CM) swam a 27.60 in the 10 & Under 50 fly, breaking the previous record of 28.35 set by Matthew Abysinghe (DR) in Feb. 2007.
  • Kelsey Morgan (DST) broke records in the 11-12 Girls 100 & 200 Breast, swimming a 1:05.71 and 2:20.00 respectively. The previous records were a 1:06.29 and 2:23.82, both set by Alison Fealey (CM) in March 1991. (Kelsey broke the 200 breast record at the Ohio Senior Meet.)
  • It was Miranda Donley (NAAC) breaking records in the 11-12 Girls 50 Free & 50 Fly, setting marks of 24.28 and 25.77 respectively. The previous 50 free record was from Zhada Fields (GTAC) at a 24.40, set July 2010. The previous 50 fly record was 25.79 set by Rachel Jackson (NAAC) in March 2008.
  • In the 11-12 Girls 400 Medley Relay a new record of 4:06.54 set by the Northern Kentucky Clippers Relay of Sophie Skinner, Sarah Harkrader, Samantha Glass, and Mallory Beil, which broke the previous record of 4:06.92 set by the Cincinnati Pepsi Marlins all the way back in December 1979 (which was the national record until 1992.)
  • The Northern Kentucky Clippers relay of Michael Summe, Jacob Lentsch, Austin Haney, and Robert Newman swam a 3:19.89 in the 400 Free Relay, which broke the old record of 3:34.89 set by the Dayon Raiders in March 2009.

Next come records set at the Sectional Championships. All of these are LONG COURSE records.

  • It was 12 year old Kelsey Morgan (DST) swimming a 2:40.63 in the 200 Breast, crushing her own 11-12 record of 2:48.34 from June of last year.
  • In the 13-14 girls 100 Back, Hannah Whiteley (RAYS) swam a 1:04.45, breaking the 1:04.97 of Emily Slabe (CFY) from July 2010.
  • Zoe Thatcher (RAYS) swam an 800 Free in 8:55.49, which broke the 15-16 record of 8:55.92 set by Margy Keefe (AB) in August 2001.
  • Caitlyn Forman (CLPR) swam a 1:02.44 100 back, breaking the 17-18 record of 1:02.52 set by Betsy Mitchell (CPM) all the way back in July 1984.
  • It was Max Williamson (CLPR) swimming 2:16.54 in the 200 Breast and 2:02.96 in the 200 IM to break 17-18 and overall Open records in both events. The previous 17-18 200 IM Record was 2:05.77 set by Nate Dusing (CM) in July 1997 and the previous Open IM record was 2:03.24 set by B. Frentos in August 1980. The previous 17-18 and Open 200 breast records were 2:18.03 set by the legendary Glenn Mills in July 1980. After 32 years, this swim removes Mills' name entirely from the Ohio record list.

Next come records from various other meets.

  • Swimming at the Kentucky High School Championships, Sharli Brady (CLPR) edged the 15-16 girls 500 Free record, swimming a 4:47.56. This broke the previous record of 4:47.79 set by Claire Dickmann (CLPR) from March 2007.
  • Swimming at the Ohio High School Championships, Erik Risolvato became the first 18 & Under in the state under the 20 second barrier in the 50 free. Erik swam a 19.62, smashing the previous 17-18 record of 20.01 set by Joe Hudepohl in February 1992.
  • At the YMCA National Championship meet, Joshua Fleagle swam a 43.41 in the 100 Free. This broke the previous 17-18 record of 43.43 set by Joe Hudepohl in February 1992.
  • Swimming at the NSCA Junior Nationals, Max Williamson (CLPR) swam a 3:49.19 in the 400 IM. This broke the 17-18 and Overall Open record of 3:49.94 set by Cory Chitwood (CLPR) in March 2007.

USA-S Athlete Protection Program - Thursday, December 8, 2011

USA Swimming is committed to raising awareness about athelte protection and prevention of abuse in sport. We are proud to partner with Praesidium, an industry expert in abuse prevention, to bring the swimming community customized and comprehensive training tools for coaches, and volunteers.

Our training tools will help you:
- Understand the scope and effects of abuse in sport
-Recognize the signs of grooming behavior and boundary violations
-Understand how to establish boundaries and protect against false allegations
-Know how to react and report when you suspect abuse

Existing 2011 non-athlete members are required to complete the USA Swimming athelte protection training course by December 31, 2011. Individuals not currently registered are required to complete the course before they can become members.

Brand new members will be required to have their non-athlete application form and payment processed by their LSC registrar before they can sign in to complete the course.

The course is free of charge and should take about an hour to complete. Click the link below to take you to the USA-Swimming cover page explaining the program and providing a link to the test:

Final Long Course LSC Records - Updated - Friday, August 19, 2011

8 more LSC Records were set at the final meet of the Long Course season. This concludes a season where there were an uncommonly large number of records set throughout the season instead of just 1 or 2 meets.

At the USA National Championships Max Williamson (CLPR) swam a 400 IM in 4:23.05 which breaks both the 15-16 record of 4:28.40 set by Kevin Nead (ABLY) in July 2002, and the overall LSC record of 4:23.26 set by J. Frentos (CPM) in June 1984.

At the Central Zone Championships, 13 year old Cooper Hodge (CAC) broke 2 LSC records while at the bottom of the age group. In the 100 Breast Cooper swam a 1:09.20. This edged out the 1:09.27 of Garth Fealy (CM) in July 1995. He also tied the 200 IM record of 2:13.59, previously set last August by Patrick Mulcare (DR).

Also at the Central Zone Championships Greg Kalin (MHSW) swam a 29.03 in his 50 free, and a 1:04.23 both of which broke his previous records set 3 weeks prior at the Ohio Long Course JO meet.

Also at the USA Junior National Championships 15 year old Patrick Mulcare (DR) set 2 new LSC records for 15-16 boys. His time of 2:05.25 in the 200 IM and 4:26.30 in the 400 IM broke the previous records of 2:05.67 (Max Williamson, CLPR, just last month) and 4:28.40 (Kevin Nead, ABLY, July 2002) respectively.

Also at the Junior National Championships Cliff Goertemiller (DR) swam a 1500 free in 15:44.69, breaking the previous record of 15:51.12 set in August 2005 by David Mosko (CM).

Many LSC records at JO Champs - Friday, July 22, 2011

In a season where there were many LSC record being set mid-season, it makes sense that there were many LSC Records broken at the Long Course JO Championships last weekend.

In the 13-14 Girls 200 Free, Cara Norris (CM) swam a 2:04.78. This broke one of the older records on the books: the 2:05.38 of Karen Gallagher (CPM) from all the way back in August of 1979!

In the 13-14 Girls 200 Back, Abby Stone (OSSC) swam a 2:19.95, which beat the 2:20.44 set by Jenny Forster (WTRC) in July, 2002.

In the 11-12 Girls 50 Free, Ashley Volpenhein (RAYS) swam a 27.68, edging out the 27.79 of Kelly Jones (KEY) from August, 1995.

In the 10 & Under boys events Greg Kalin (MHSW) broke several records.

  • In the 50 Free, his 29.58 beat the record of 29.69, which he set earlier in the season.
  • In the 50 Fly, Greg swam a 31.82 beating the previous record of 32.66 set by Sam Mitchell (WTRC) in August 2004.
  • In the 100 Free, he swam a 1:05.24, which beats the 1:05.46 of Sam Mitchell (WTRC) in August 2004.

In the relays, The Cincinnati Marlins 13-14 boys 200 Medley Relay of Matt MacDonald, Grant Carr, Mitchell Frey, and Derek Roth dropped the record to a 1:55.75 from the previous 1:56.30, also set by the Marlins in July 1995.

 

New National Age Group Record - Updated - Tuesday, July 12, 2011

The Greater Toledo Aquatic Club Relay of Catherine Kight, Katie Kreger, Brea Fields, and Kelly Metzger broke the 10 & Under Girls 200 Free Relay National Age Group Record this weekend at the Mike Peppe/John Bruce Memorial Inviational at The Ohio State University. The girls split 30.22, 32.43, 31.79 and 31.58 respectively. This breaks the previous National Record of 2:06.46 set in 2005 by Blue Tide Aquatics from the Gulf LSC in Texas.

Swimming at the Western Great Lakes Open in Brown Deer, WI the same girls lowered the record to 2:05.70. At the same meet, Catherine Kight (GTAC) swam a 29.98 in the 50 Free. This betters the previous 10 & Under girls record of 30.06, set by Kelly Jones (KEY) in July 1993.
